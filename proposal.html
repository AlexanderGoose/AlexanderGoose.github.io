Computational Analysis of Mountain Lake Ecology at Castle Lake

Alexander Gustafson

Vision Statement

This project is motivated by the desire to learn how to use computer programming to investigate ecological data. This project will encourage me to learn more complex programming with R as well as data cleaning, mining, analysis, and visualization. All of these skills I have learned, mostly in theory, but I have not had to implement them to this extent. This project will broadly focus on finding interesting patterns using a long term study of Castle Lake in Northern California, a glacial lake sitting above 5,500 feet.

Motivation

This project is important to me because I have a deep interest in high alpine ecology, particularly glacial lakes and tundras. My focus centers on the questions of how warming temperatures are influencing the ecological composition of these lakes. More specific goals will be an attempt to find major disturbances and determine their impact on lake ecology. This could include sudden changes in water temperature and its impact on phytoplankton communities. Further, I hope to create a long term analysis that attempts to find how rising air and water temperatures are influencing lake composition including nutrients, dissolved inorganic and organic carbon, and plankton concentrations.

The dataset I am using, Mountain Lake Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and Climate Data since 1959 at Castle Lake (Chandra et al., 2022), includes roughly 50 years of data that comes from sampling taken every five days at various depths. These samples include water temperatures between the years 1959 to 2020, chlorophyll concentrations, and nutrient levels at various depths from 1968 to 2020.
